Introduction to electronics

It seems impossible to imagine   life without electronics. Smartphones, computers, home   appliances,  security   systems, and   transit   systems all run on electrical circuits. But electronics have a long history predating modern times. In fact, the foundation for modern electronics dates back to 1897, when J.A. Fleming   invented   the vacuum diode, and later the vacuum triode to amplify electrical signals.

As daily life becomes increasingly   intertwined with electronic  devices, electronics and electrical engineering are particularly relevant fields that can be exciting and fulfilling to explore. These fields deal primarily with the transmission of electrical power, whether it’s going into a computer’s semiconductor or traveling through local power lines.
